# Donation / "How this is funded" — strategy + copy

You asked about a donation page at the end of onboarding for this open, at-cost, reinvested project.
The instinct is good and fits the honest ethos of the whole Bible. Two changes make it work far better.

## 1. Move it out of onboarding

Asking for money at the *end of onboarding* is the classic conversion mistake: the user hasn't received
any value yet — they haven't even trained once. To a brand-new user, a money ask there can read like a
soft paywall or bait-and-switch, and it adds friction exactly where you want zero.

**Instead:**
- Keep onboarding **100% donation-free** — its only job is to get the user to their first workout.
- Provide a calm, always-reachable **"How this is funded"** page (settings/menu).
- Prompt for support only **after a genuine win** — first completed week, a new PR, one month in —
  always **skippable**, **never gated**. Post-value asks convert better *and* feel earned.

## 2. Word "non-profit" carefully — it's a legal term

Unless you've registered a non-profit/charity, don't call it a "non-profit organization" or imply
donations are tax-deductible. Say **"a not-for-profit passion project — I run it at cost and put every
donation back in."** True and safe.

## 3. Use a processor that doesn't eat small donations

Stripe's ~2.9% + 30¢ is brutal on a $3 gift (nets ~$2.60). Better fits:

| Option | Why it fits this project |
|--------|--------------------------|
| **Open Collective** | *Best fit* — publicly displays real income **and expenses**, which literally *is* your "explain the cost" goal, done automatically and credibly |
| **GitHub Sponsors** | 0% fees; sits next to the open-source repo |
| **Ko-fi / Liberapay** | Low/zero fees; simple one-off or recurring |

## 4. Be honest about the (low) cost

On the [Cloudflare stack](hosting.md), early costs are **near-zero** — don't inflate them. "Right now
this runs for about the price of a domain name; here's what scales the bill as we grow" is more
trustworthy than a padded number, and it makes the eventual asks credible.

## Draft copy — "How this is funded" (reachable page, not gated)

> ## This project is free, and I'd like to keep it that way
>
> The Hypertrophy Bible is a passion project, not a business. The knowledge base and the app are
> **open-source**, every claim is backed by a **real study**, and I'm not trying to sell you anything —
> **no ads, no "premium tier," no selling your data.**
>
> **What it costs.** Right now, roughly the price of a domain name — most of it runs on free
> infrastructure. As more people use it, the bill grows (compute, storage, a domain). I cover it myself
> and reinvest anything I receive straight back into the project.
>
> **If it's helped you** and you'd like to chip in, you can — completely optional, one-off or recurring,
> any amount. **You get the exact same app either way.**
>
> → **[Support the project]**
>
> *Every dollar in and out is public on Open Collective, so you can see exactly where it goes.*

## Draft copy — the gentle post-milestone nudge (skippable, shown after a real win)

> 🎉 **Nice — that's your first month done.**
> This app is free and always will be. If it's been useful and you'd like to help cover the running
> costs, you can chip in any amount — totally optional. *[Maybe later]  ·  [Support the project]*

## Placement summary

| Surface | Money ask? |
|---------|------------|
| Onboarding | **Never** |
| Settings / menu → "How this is funded" | Always available, informational |
| After a milestone (first week/month, a PR) | Gentle, skippable nudge |
| Anywhere else | No |
