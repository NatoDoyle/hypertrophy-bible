# Training Splits

> **TL;DR** — A "split" is just how you divide your muscles across your training days. There is **no magic split** — the best one is whatever lets you hit each muscle's **weekly volume** at a **frequency of ~2×** with **quality sets**, given how many days you can train. Full-body, upper/lower, and push/pull/legs are all excellent; the classic one-muscle-per-day "bro split" is the weakest default only because it caps each muscle at ~1×/week.

**Quick recommendations**
- Choose a split by **days available**: 3 days → full-body or upper/lower; 4 → upper/lower; 5–6 → push/pull/legs or upper/lower hybrids. **[Grade C]**
- Aim for each muscle **~2×/week**; this usually beats 1× at matched volume by keeping set quality high. **[Grade B]**
- Don't obsess over the split name — **weekly volume and effort** matter far more. **[Grade A]**

## Practical Application

| Days/week | Good splits | Typical per-muscle frequency |
|-----------|-------------|------------------------------|
| 3 | Full-body, or upper/lower/full | ~2–3× |
| 4 | Upper/lower (×2) | ~2× |
| 5 | Upper/lower + push/pull/legs hybrid | ~2× |
| 6 | Push/pull/legs (×2) | ~2× |

**The split is a delivery vehicle for volume and frequency.** Pick the total weekly volume per muscle you want (see [Volume Progression & Deloads](volume-progression-and-deloads.md)), then choose a structure that spreads it across ~2 sessions per muscle without any single session becoming a quality-killing marathon. A "bro split" (chest day, back day, etc.) forces all of a muscle's volume into one weekly session, which is why it's the weakest general choice — not because it's useless.

## The Evidence

Splits matter through **frequency and volume distribution**, both already established. Training a muscle **~2×/week tends to beat 1×** — largely because the same volume is done with fresher, higher-quality sets[^schoenfeld-2016-frequency-meta] **[Grade B]** — while at **matched weekly volume, frequency itself has little independent effect**[^schoenfeld-2019-frequency-meta]. **[Grade A]** So the split's job is simply to let you accumulate sufficient weekly [volume](../01-training-variables/volume.md), the true driver of growth[^schoenfeld-2017-volume-dose-response]. **[Grade A]** No split has a special hypertrophy property beyond how it arranges these variables.

## Key Uncertainties & Nuance
- **Adherence dominates.** The split you'll do consistently beats the "optimal" one you skip.
- **Recovery between overlapping days** matters — e.g. back-to-back push and pull days can leave shoulders/triceps under-recovered; sequence thoughtfully.
- **Very high volumes push you toward higher frequency** (3×+ per muscle), because they can't fit into two quality sessions.
