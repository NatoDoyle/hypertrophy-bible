# Chest (Pectoralis Major)

> **TL;DR** — The chest is one muscle with two functional regions — an **upper (clavicular)** and a **lower/mid (sternal)** head — plus it assists shoulder movement. Train it with a **press** and at least one **fly/adduction** movement, include an **incline** angle to bias the upper chest, and emphasize the **stretched (bottom) position**. Roughly **8–20 hard sets per week**, ~2× per week, covers most people.

**Quick recommendations**
- Use **2–3 exercises**: a flat/decline press, an incline press, and a fly or cable crossover. **[Grade C]**
- Add an **incline** movement — pressing flat under-emphasizes the upper chest. **[Grade C]**
- Emphasize the **deep stretch** (dumbbells/machines allow more range than a barbell). **[Grade B]**
- Weekly volume: start ~**8–10 sets**, progress toward ~**12–18**, cap near ~**20–22** ([`data/muscles/chest.json`](../../data/muscles/chest.json)). **[Grade C]**

## Practical Application

**Anatomy that matters for training.** The pectoralis major has fibers running at different angles: the **clavicular head** (upper) is best challenged with the arm moving up-and-across (incline press, low-to-high cable fly), while the **sternal head** (mid/lower) dominates flat and decline pressing and horizontal adduction. Because pressing angle shifts emphasis, covering the chest well means covering angles.

| Role | Good picks |
|------|-----------|
| Lower/mid chest (bulk) | Flat/decline barbell or dumbbell press, [flat dumbbell press](../../data/exercises/dumbbell-bench-press.json) |
| Upper chest | Incline dumbbell/barbell press, low-to-high cable fly |
| Stretch & adduction | Deep dumbbell fly, cable crossover |

**Selection principles.** Favor presses that let you reach a deep stretch (dumbbells and many machines beat a barbell, which the chest stops short of); use flyes/crossovers to add adduction and loaded stretch that pressing alone misses.

## The Evidence

Chest development follows the general training-variable evidence rather than chest-specific magic. **Volume drives growth** within a recoverable range[^schoenfeld-2017-volume-dose-response] **[Grade A]**, and **range of motion — especially loading the stretched position — matters**, so deep presses and flyes are favored over short-range work[^schoenfeld-2020-rom-review][^kassiano-2023-rom-review]. **[Grade B]** The case for training multiple angles rests on regional emphasis (upper vs lower fibers) and is supported more by anatomy and electromyography than by long-term hypertrophy trials, so it's graded as reasoned practice. **[Grade C]** Underlying all of it, mechanical tension on the pec fibers is the driver[^schoenfeld-2010-mechanisms]. **[Grade C]**

## Key Uncertainties & Nuance
- **How much "upper chest" work you need** is individual; some people's clavicular head is well-served by incline pressing alone.
- **Fly vs press "peak contraction"** benefits are debated — the reliable value of flyes is the loaded stretch, not the squeeze at the top.
- **Decline pressing** offers little most people can't get from flat/incline work.
