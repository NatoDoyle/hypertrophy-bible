# Back (Lats, Upper Back & Spinal Erectors)

> **TL;DR** — "Back" is several muscles with different jobs: the **lats** (big, wing-like, pull the arms down and back), the **upper back** (traps and rhomboids, retract and elevate the shoulder blades), and the **spinal erectors** (extend and brace the spine). Train it with **both a vertical pull** (pulldown/pull-up) **and a horizontal pull** (row), emphasize the **stretch**, and let the back's high work tolerance carry relatively **high weekly volume**.

**Quick recommendations**
- Do at least **one vertical and one horizontal pull** every week. **[Grade C]**
- Emphasize the **stretched position** (full overhead reach on pulldowns; full protraction on rows). **[Grade B]**
- Lats/upper back tolerate **~10–22+ hard sets/week**; erectors need far less direct work. **[Grade C]**
- Use **chest-supported rows** when you want to train the back hard without lower-back fatigue limiting you. **[Grade C]**

## Practical Application

| Muscle | Job | Best picks |
|--------|-----|-----------|
| Lats | Shoulder extension & adduction (pull arms down/back) | [Lat pulldown](../../data/exercises/lat-pulldown.json), pull-ups, single-arm rows |
| Upper back (traps/rhomboids) | Scapular retraction/elevation | [Chest-supported row](../../data/exercises/chest-supported-row.json), cable rows, shrugs |
| Spinal erectors | Spinal extension & bracing | Deadlifts, back extensions (much comes indirectly from compounds) |

**Vertical + horizontal.** Vertical pulls (pulldown, pull-up) bias the lats through shoulder adduction; horizontal pulls (rows) bias the mid-back and also hit the lats. Doing both covers width and thickness. **Grip and path** change emphasis subtly, but the reliable rule is: full stretch at the start, full contraction to the torso, minimal torso momentum.

**Erectors need little direct work** for most people because squats, deadlifts, rows, and carries already load them heavily; add back extensions only if they lag or you want dedicated work.

## The Evidence

Back growth follows the general principles: **volume within a recoverable range drives hypertrophy**[^schoenfeld-2017-volume-dose-response] **[Grade A]**, and **loading the lengthened position** (full overhead stretch on pulldowns, full reach on rows) is favored by the range-of-motion evidence[^schoenfeld-2020-rom-review]. **[Grade B]** The specific value of training multiple pulling angles is grounded in the muscles' distinct functions and is standard practice, but direct hypertrophy comparisons of pull variations are limited, so it's graded accordingly. **[Grade C]** Chest support raises the value of rows for hypertrophy by removing the lower-back and momentum limitations that otherwise cap effort. **[Grade C]**

## Key Uncertainties & Nuance
- **The lats are hard to "feel"** for many lifters; a deliberate stretch and controlled pull matter more than chasing a mind-muscle cue.
- **Rows train a mix** of lats, mid-traps, rhomboids, and rear delts; exact partitioning depends on elbow path and grip.
- **Deadlifts build the back and erectors** but are systemically fatiguing — count them in your weekly volume rather than adding them on top of everything.
