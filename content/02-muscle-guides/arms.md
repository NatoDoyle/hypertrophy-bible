# Arms (Biceps, Triceps & Forearms)

> **TL;DR** — The **triceps** make up ~two-thirds of the upper arm, so if size is the goal, don't neglect them. Both biceps and triceps get **indirect work** (biceps from pulling, triceps from pressing), so direct-work needs are moderate. The highest-yield tweak for both is **training the long heads in a stretched position** — **overhead** for the triceps long head, **behind-the-body** (incline) for the biceps long head. Forearms grow from grip-intensive work plus a little direct wrist work.

**Quick recommendations**
- Include an **overhead triceps extension** to load the long head at length. **[Grade B]**
- Include a **stretch-biased curl** (incline or overhead) for the biceps long head. **[Grade B]**
- Direct volume: biceps ~**8–20**, triceps ~**6–18** sets/week, *on top of* pulling/pressing. **[Grade C]**
- Train forearms with **loaded carries/heavy holds** plus wrist curls if lagging. **[Grade C]**

## Practical Application

| Muscle | Function | Best picks |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Biceps (+ brachialis) | Elbow flexion, forearm supination | [Incline dumbbell curl](../../data/exercises/incline-dumbbell-curl.json), preacher curl, hammer curl (brachialis) |
| Triceps | Elbow extension; long head also shoulder extension | [Overhead cable extension](../../data/exercises/overhead-cable-triceps-extension.json), pushdowns, close-grip press |
| Forearms | Wrist flexion/extension, grip | Wrist curls/extensions, heavy carries, reverse curls |

**Stretch the long heads.** The triceps long head and biceps long head both cross the shoulder, so their stretch depends on shoulder position: put the arm **overhead** to stretch the triceps long head, and **behind the torso** (incline bench) to stretch the biceps long head. These positions are where the biggest selection gains come from.

**Mind the overlap.** Because rows/pulldowns train the biceps and presses train the triceps, count some of that as fractional arm volume — you usually need less isolated arm work than it seems.

## The Evidence

The strongest arm-specific finding is about **exercise position**: Maeo and colleagues (2023) found **substantially greater triceps growth training elbow extensions overhead versus with the arm at the side**, because the overhead position loads the long head at long length[^maeo-2023-triceps-overhead]. **[Grade B]** This generalizes via the broader lengthened-position evidence to the biceps long head (incline/behind-the-body curls)[^pedrosa-2022-lengthened-partials]. **[Grade B]** Beyond position, arm growth follows **volume**[^schoenfeld-2017-volume-dose-response] **[Grade A]** and can use **any load taken close to failure**, so lighter, higher-rep isolation work is perfectly effective[^schoenfeld-2017-load-meta]. **[Grade A]**

## Key Uncertainties & Nuance
- **Biceps "peak"** is largely genetic (muscle belly length/insertions); you can grow the muscle but not reshape its fundamental form.
- **Brachialis** development (under the biceps) adds arm thickness and is trained by neutral/pronated-grip curls — a worthwhile inclusion.
- **Forearm/grip** work overlaps heavily with heavy pulling; dedicated work is optional unless grip or forearm size is a specific goal.
