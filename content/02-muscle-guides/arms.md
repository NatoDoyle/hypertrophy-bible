# Arms (Biceps, Triceps & Forearms)

> **TL;DR** — The **triceps** make up ~two-thirds of the upper arm, so if size is the goal, don't neglect them. Both biceps and triceps get **indirect work** (biceps from pulling, triceps from pressing), so direct-work needs are moderate. For the **triceps**, training the long head in a **stretched (overhead)** position grows it more — that's a direct finding. For the **biceps**, the once-assumed "stretch the long head with incline curls to grow it more" has now been tested directly and **didn't hold up** once the resistance profile is matched, so pick curls by comfort and loading, not by chasing shoulder position. Forearms grow from grip-intensive work plus a little direct wrist work.

**Quick recommendations**
- Include an **overhead triceps extension** to load the long head at length. **[Grade B]**
- Curl variety is fine, but **don't expect a stretch-biased (incline/behind-the-body) curl to out-grow a standing one** — two direct biceps trials found no hypertrophy advantage from the shoulder-extended position once resistance profiles are matched. **[Grade B]** Choose curls by loading and joint comfort.
- Direct volume: biceps ~**8–20**, triceps ~**6–18** sets/week, *on top of* pulling/pressing. **[Grade C]**
- Train forearms with **loaded carries/heavy holds** plus wrist curls if lagging. **[Grade C]**

## Practical Application

| Muscle | Function | Best picks |
|--------|----------|-----------|
| Biceps (+ brachialis) | Elbow flexion, forearm supination | [Incline dumbbell curl](../../data/exercises/incline-dumbbell-curl.json), preacher curl, hammer curl (brachialis) |
| Triceps | Elbow extension; long head also shoulder extension | [Overhead cable extension](../../data/exercises/overhead-cable-triceps-extension.json), pushdowns, close-grip press |
| Forearms | Wrist flexion/extension, grip | Wrist curls/extensions, heavy carries, reverse curls |

**Stretch the triceps long head.** The triceps long head crosses the shoulder, so put the arm **overhead** to load it at long length — that's the position where the direct evidence shows a real selection gain. The biceps long head also crosses the shoulder, but stretching it behind the torso (incline curls) did **not** produce extra growth in direct trials once the resistance profile was matched (see The Evidence) — so treat incline/behind-the-body curls as a comfortable, well-loaded option, not a growth hack.

**Mind the overlap.** Because rows/pulldowns train the biceps and presses train the triceps, count some of that as fractional arm volume — you usually need less isolated arm work than it seems.

## The Evidence

The strongest arm-specific finding is about **exercise position**: Maeo and colleagues (2023) found **substantially greater triceps growth training elbow extensions overhead versus with the arm at the side**, because the overhead position loads the long head at long length[^maeo-2023-triceps-overhead]. **[Grade B]** It was long assumed the same logic would carry to the **biceps long head** (which crosses the shoulder, so a behind-the-body/incline curl lengthens it), but that has now been tested directly and the assumption **did not survive**. Attarieh and colleagues (2025) compared preacher curls (shoulder flexed, biceps short) with Bayesian cable curls (shoulder extended, biceps long) **matched for resistance profile** and found the shoulder angle "does not seem to affect muscle hypertrophy," with equivalent growth between conditions[^attarieh-2025-biceps-shoulder-angle]. Larsen and colleagues (2026), comparing a neutral versus maximally extended shoulder in the cable curl with resistance profile and elbow ROM matched, likewise found that "altering shoulder extension angle in isolation does not appear to produce meaningful differences in elbow flexor hypertrophy," with Bayes factors favoring the null[^larsen-2026-biceps-shoulder-angle]. **[Grade B]** The read: the **biceps is an exception** to the triceps/hamstring lengthened-position pattern — once the load-through-range (resistance profile) is matched, where the shoulder sits doesn't add growth. Any apparent edge of one curl over another is about **where in the range it loads you hardest**, not muscle length per se. Beyond position, arm growth follows **volume**[^schoenfeld-2017-volume-dose-response] **[Grade A]** and can use **any load taken close to failure**, so lighter, higher-rep isolation work is perfectly effective[^schoenfeld-2017-load-meta]. **[Grade A]**

## Key Uncertainties & Nuance
- **Biceps "peak"** is largely genetic (muscle belly length/insertions); you can grow the muscle but not reshape its fundamental form.
- **Brachialis** development (under the biceps) adds arm thickness and is trained by neutral/pronated-grip curls — a worthwhile inclusion.
- **Forearm/grip** work overlaps heavily with heavy pulling; dedicated work is optional unless grip or forearm size is a specific goal.
