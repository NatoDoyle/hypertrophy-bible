# Pillar 01 — Training Variables

The dials you turn in a program, and what the evidence says about each. This is the **exemplar
pillar** — built first and to full depth, it sets the quality bar (structure, citations, grading) for
every other pillar in the Bible.

**Status:** ✅ Complete

> **TL;DR** — For hypertrophy, the variables that matter most, roughly in order: do enough **volume**
> (~10+ hard sets per muscle per week, individualized), train each muscle with **effort** (close to
> failure, ~0–4 reps in reserve), across a **rep range** that is broad (~5–30 reps grows muscle
> similarly when effort matches), spread over a **frequency** that lets you accumulate that volume with
> good quality (often 2× per week), using a **full range of motion** (with a likely edge for
> long-muscle-length work), and **rest** long enough (~2 min+) to perform your sets well. Tempo and
> exercise order matter least. Details, numbers, and evidence grades are on each page below.

## How the variables relate

Volume, intensity, and frequency are not independent knobs — they trade off. **Frequency is largely a
tool to distribute volume**; **load and rep range are flexible as long as sets are taken close enough
to failure**; and **more volume helps only up to the point you can recover from it**. The job of a
program (Pillar 03) is to set these dials so total *effective, recoverable* volume is high. Read the
Volume and Proximity-to-failure pages first — they carry the most weight.
