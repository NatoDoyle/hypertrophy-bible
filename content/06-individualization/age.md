# Age

> **TL;DR** — You can build muscle at essentially **any age** — older adults gain muscle and strength from resistance training, and it's one of the most valuable things they can do for health and independence. Older lifters may need **more attention to recovery, joints, and per-meal protein**, and respond well to **adequate (not minimal) volume**. Adolescents can train safely with good coaching and technique. Age changes the details, not the fundamentals.

**Quick recommendations**
- **Older adults**: resistance-train — it builds muscle/strength and protects function; use adequate volume, manage joints and recovery. **[Grade A]**
- Older lifters may need **more protein per meal** (~0.4+ g/kg) to overcome anabolic resistance (see [Protein](../04-nutrition/protein.md)). **[Grade B]**
- **Adolescents** can strength-train safely with supervision, technique focus, and sensible loads. **[Grade C]**
- Expect **slower recovery** with age — prioritize sleep and don't over-stack systemic fatigue. **[Grade C]**

## Practical Application

**Older adults (masters lifters).** Resistance training reverses much of age-related muscle loss (sarcopenia), improving strength, function, and quality of life. Key adjustments: emphasize **recovery** (recovery capacity declines with age), favor **joint-friendly exercise selection** (machines and controlled ranges as needed), keep **protein high with adequate per-meal doses**, and use **sufficient volume** — older adults respond to real training, not just token effort. Progress may be a bit slower, but the trajectory is upward.

**Adolescents/youth.** Contrary to old myths, supervised resistance training is safe for young people and doesn't stunt growth. The emphasis should be on **technique, movement skill, and gradual progression** rather than maximal loads. Visible hypertrophy is more limited before/around puberty but strength and skill develop well.

## The Evidence

**Older adults build muscle with sufficient training volume.** Radaelli and colleagues (2025) meta-analyzed resistance-training volume in older adults and found **resistance training improves lean mass, lower-body hypertrophy, strength, and physical function**, with higher volumes generally supporting greater lower-body outcomes[^radaelli-2025-older-adults-volume]. **[Grade A]** The practical message: older adults shouldn't be prescribed only minimal "senior" training — they respond to genuine, progressive resistance training with adequate volume, while managing recovery and joints.

## Key Uncertainties & Nuance
- **Recovery slows with age**, so the *same* high volume may need to be distributed with more rest; individualize.
- **Anabolic resistance** means older muscle responds a bit less to a given protein dose — higher per-meal protein helps compensate.
- **Youth hypertrophy is hormone-limited** pre-puberty; strength/skill gains are the main early adaptations.
- **Health screening** is prudent for older or previously-sedentary beginners — this Bible is not medical advice.
