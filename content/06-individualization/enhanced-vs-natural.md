# Enhanced vs Natural (and Hormones)

> **TL;DR** — The **single biggest reason** some physiques aren't naturally attainable is **anabolic steroids and other performance-enhancing drugs (PEDs)** — which dramatically increase muscle, even *without* training. This matters for you in three ways: (1) **comparing yourself to enhanced physiques** (many elite bodybuilders and physique influencers) sets false expectations; (2) **acute, natural training-induced hormone spikes are NOT the same thing** and don't meaningfully build muscle; and (3) **natural muscular potential has real limits**. This page is educational — it is **not an endorsement or a how-to**; PEDs carry serious health and legal risks.

**Quick recommendations**
- Judge your progress against **natural benchmarks and your own trajectory** — not against likely-enhanced physiques. **[Grade B]**
- Don't chase "hormone-boosting" workouts or supplements — **acute natural hormone spikes don't drive growth**. **[Grade B]**
- Set expectations around **realistic natural limits** (e.g. an FFMI around the mid-20s as a rough ceiling). **[Grade C]**
- Understand PEDs are **health-risky and often illegal** — this Bible covers natural, evidence-based training. **[Grade D]**

## Practical Application

**Why this belongs in a training guide.** Not because you should use drugs — but because **not understanding their role wrecks people's expectations and self-image**. A huge fraction of the physiques presented as "natural results of a program/supplement" are chemically assisted. Chasing those results naturally leads to frustration, over-training, disordered eating, and falling for scam supplements marketed against an impossible standard.

**What to do with this:**
- **Recalibrate comparisons.** Benchmark against verified natural lifters and, above all, your own past self (see [Tracking → Assessing Progress](../07-tracking/assessing-progress.md)).
- **Ignore hormone-optimization marketing.** Programs and supplements promising to "boost testosterone" for muscle are exploiting a myth (see below and [Myths](../08-myths/index.md)).
- **Respect natural potential.** Genetics set your ceiling and rate (see [Genetics](genetics-and-anthropometry.md)); a rough natural upper bound for muscularity exists, and approaching it takes years of consistent work.
- **If considering PEDs**, understand they carry cardiovascular, hormonal, psychological, and other risks and legal consequences — that's a medical decision, not a training tip, and outside this Bible's scope.

## The Evidence

**Exogenous androgens build muscle powerfully — even without training.** Bhasin and colleagues' landmark 1996 trial gave men **supraphysiologic testosterone**, and the testosterone groups **gained substantial muscle size and strength**; strikingly, the group given testosterone **without exercise gained more muscle than natural trainees who exercised without it**[^bhasin-1996-testosterone]. **[Grade B]** This is why enhanced physiques aren't a fair yardstick for natural lifters.

**But natural training hormone spikes are a different thing entirely.** The transient rises in testosterone and growth hormone from a workout do **not** meaningfully drive hypertrophy — Morton and colleagues found **neither load nor the post-exercise hormonal response determined muscle growth**[^morton-2016-load-hormones]. **[Grade B]** Conflating "steroids build muscle" with "my workout spikes testosterone, so hormone-boosting workouts build muscle" is a category error (see [Timing & Hormone Myths](../08-myths/timing-and-hormone-myths.md)).

**Natural potential has a rough ceiling.** Kouri and colleagues (1995) found that non-users of anabolic steroids clustered below a **fat-free mass index (FFMI) of about 25**, while users commonly exceeded it — a widely-cited (if imperfect) heuristic for a natural muscularity limit[^kouri-1995-ffmi]. **[Grade C]**

## Key Uncertainties & Nuance
- **FFMI ~25 is a heuristic, not a hard law** — exceptional genetics, height, and measurement error create exceptions; it's a rough guide to set expectations, not a verdict on any individual.
- **"Natural" claims are unverifiable** for most public figures — assume many impressive physiques are assisted rather than assuming they're natural.
- **Being in the normal testosterone range matters; small natural fluctuations don't** — chasing minor endogenous hormone changes is not a productive lever.
- **This is educational, not prescriptive** — PED use is a serious medical/legal matter beyond this Bible's remit, and nothing here should be read as encouragement.
