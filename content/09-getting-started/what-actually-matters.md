# What Actually Matters (In Priority Order)

> **TL;DR** — Almost everyone new to lifting worries about the wrong things — the "best" exercise, the perfect split, tempo, supplements, meal timing — while under-doing the few things that actually build muscle. Here is the honest order of what matters, biggest lever first: **(1) showing up consistently, (2) doing enough hard sets, (3) training those sets close to failure, (4) eating enough protein and the right calories, (5) sleeping enough, then (6) frequency, (7) adding a little over time, and (8) exercise choice and full range of motion.** Everything below that — tempo, exact rest times, exercise order, most supplements — is rounding-error until the top of the list is dialed in. Get the big rocks right and you cannot help but grow.

**Quick recommendations**
- **Nail consistency first.** The program you actually follow for months beats the "optimal" one you quit in three weeks. If a choice threatens your adherence, adherence wins. **[Grade D]**
- **Do enough hard volume** — about **10+ hard sets per muscle per week** is the single biggest training lever. **[Grade A]**
- **Train those sets close to failure** (roughly 0–4 reps in reserve). Junk-effort sets far from failure barely count. **[Grade A]**
- **Eat enough protein** (~1.6 g/kg/day is plenty) and set **calories to your goal** (slight surplus to gain, deficit to lose). **[Grade A]**
- **Sleep enough** — you grow between sessions, not during them. **[Grade B]**
- **Stop optimizing the small stuff** (tempo, exact rest, exercise order, fat-burner supplements, meal timing) until everything above is consistently in place. **[Grade C]**

## The order that matters

Think of it as a ladder. Each rung only pays off once the ones below it are solid — a perfect tempo on a workout you skipped is worth nothing.

1. **Consistency / adherence — the foundation.** More than any program detail, *doing the work repeatedly over months* is what builds muscle. This is why the app is built around one simple screen a day. Habits first: see [building the training habit](building-the-training-habit.md). **[Grade D]**
2. **Enough hard volume.** Weekly hard sets per muscle is the variable most tightly tied to growth, with a clear dose-response up to what you can recover from. Aim for the productive range rather than the bare minimum. See [Volume](../01-training-variables/volume.md). **[Grade A]**
3. **Real effort (proximity to failure).** Those sets have to be *hard* — taken close to failure. The exact number of reps you leave in reserve matters less than genuinely pushing. See [Proximity to failure](../01-training-variables/proximity-to-failure.md). **[Grade A]**
4. **Protein and calories.** Muscle is built from protein and needs energy. ~1.6 g/kg/day of protein covers it, and your calorie balance sets whether you gain, hold, or lose. See [Protein](../04-nutrition/protein.md) and [Energy balance](../04-nutrition/energy-balance.md). **[Grade A]**
5. **Sleep and recovery.** Training is only the stimulus; growth happens during recovery, and sleep is its biggest input. See [Sleep](../05-recovery/sleep.md) and [stimulus, fatigue & adaptation](../00-foundations/stimulus-fatigue-adaptation.md). **[Grade B]**
6. **Frequency.** How often you train a muscle mostly matters as a *tool to fit your volume* in with good quality — often about twice a week, but it's a lever for the rungs above, not a goal itself. See [Frequency](../01-training-variables/frequency.md). **[Grade B]**
7. **Progressive overload over time.** Gradually do a bit more — a rep, a little weight, an occasional set — so your muscles keep having a reason to adapt. See [periodization & progression](../03-programming/periodization-and-progression.md). **[Grade B]**
8. **Exercise selection and range of motion.** Now it's worth picking exercises you can load and progress, and training them through a full range. Important, but far down the list — a great exercise done inconsistently and easily still loses to a "good enough" one done hard and often. See [exercise selection & order](../01-training-variables/exercise-selection-and-order.md) and [range of motion](../01-training-variables/range-of-motion.md). **[Grade B]**

## What to basically ignore (for now)

None of these are *useless* — they're just so far down the list that fussing over them while the top rungs are shaky is wasted energy:

- **Tempo / how slow you lift each rep** — control the weight, don't count seconds. See [tempo](../01-training-variables/tempo-and-rep-duration.md).
- **Exact rest times** — rest long enough to do the next set well; the stopwatch isn't the point. See [rest periods](../01-training-variables/rest-periods.md).
- **Exercise order and "muscle confusion"** — minor, and mostly marketing.
- **Supplements beyond creatine** — most do little; creatine is the rare cheap, well-evidenced one. See [supplements](../04-nutrition/supplements.md).
- **Advanced intensity techniques** (drop sets, myo-reps) — fine seasoning once the basics are heavy and consistent, not a substitute for them. See [advanced techniques](../01-training-variables/advanced-techniques.md).
- **Precise meal timing** — total daily protein and calories dwarf *when* you eat.

## The app already does the ordering for you

You don't have to hold this whole ladder in your head. The plan sets your **volume**, **effort targets**, **frequency**, and **progression** from the science, prefills your weights, and adjusts them from how you actually respond over time — so the top rungs are handled by default. Your job is rung #1: **show up and do the sets.** Everything else is already ordered for you.
