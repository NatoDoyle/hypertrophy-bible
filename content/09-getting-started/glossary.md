# Glossary

> **TL;DR** — This is your dictionary for the whole Bible. Every gym word you'll bump into is defined here in one or two plain sentences, grouped by topic so you can find it fast. You don't need to memorise any of it — just come back when a term stops you. When a word matters enough to have its own page, we link to it.

**Quick recommendations**
- **Skim to the term you need** using the group headers below, or search the page (Ctrl/Cmd-F).
- **Bookmark this page.** You'll re-read it for the first month, and that's normal.
- Remember one thing above all: in this Bible, a **"set" means a hard working set** taken close to failure — warm-up sets don't count. **[Grade D]**

## The basics

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Rep** (repetition) | One complete lift-and-lower of an exercise — e.g. lowering into a squat and standing back up is one rep. |
| **Set** | A group of reps done back-to-back without resting — "3 sets of 8" means 8 reps, rest, 8 more, rest, 8 more. |
| **Working set** | A set done for real, close to failure (see below). These are the sets that build muscle and the ones we count. |
| **Warm-up set** | A lighter, easier set done first to prepare your body. It primes you but doesn't count toward your muscle-building work. |
| **Range of motion (ROM)** | How far a joint travels during a rep. "Full ROM" means the complete movement, e.g. a squat all the way down and all the way up. |
| **Tempo** | How fast you move through a rep — how many seconds you spend lowering, pausing, and lifting the weight. |

## How hard you push

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Training to failure** | Doing reps until you physically cannot complete another one with good form. |
| **RIR (Reps In Reserve)** | How many more reps you *could* have done before failing. "2 RIR" means you stopped with about 2 reps left in the tank. |
| **RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion)** | A 1–10 effort score that's the flip side of RIR: RPE 8 ≈ 2 RIR, RPE 9 ≈ 1 RIR, RPE 10 = true failure. |
| **1RM (one-rep max)** | The heaviest weight you can lift for a single rep. |
| **%1RM** | A weight expressed as a percentage of your 1RM — e.g. 80% of a 100 kg max is 80 kg. |
| **AMRAP** | "As Many Reps As Possible" — a set where you do all the good reps you can rather than a fixed number. |

## Exercise types and techniques

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Compound exercise** | A move that works several muscles across multiple joints — squat, bench press, row, pull-up. |
| **Isolation exercise** | A move that targets mainly one muscle at one joint — bicep curl, leg extension, lateral raise. |
| **Superset** | Two exercises done back-to-back with little or no rest between them. |
| **Drop set** | Finishing a set at failure, immediately reducing the weight, and repping out again — an intensity technique, not an everyday tool. |

## Progress and results

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Progressive overload** | Gradually doing more over time — more weight, more reps, or more sets — so your muscles keep having a reason to grow. |
| **PR (personal record)** | Your own best-ever performance on a lift, e.g. a new heaviest weight or most reps. |
| **Hypertrophy** | Muscle growth — the increase in muscle size that this whole site is about. |
| **Strength** | How much force you can produce, i.e. how heavy you can lift. Related to size but not the same goal. |
| **DOMS (Delayed Onset Muscle Soreness)** | The muscle ache that shows up a day or two after training. Normal, and **not** a required sign of a good workout. |

## Programming words

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Volume** | How much work you do, usually counted as **hard sets per muscle per week**. |
| **Effective sets (why you might see a half)** | A set counts as **1 for the main muscle** it trains and **½ for each assisting muscle**. Bench press is a chest exercise, but your triceps and front delts help — so one hard bench set is 1 set for chest and ½ a set each for triceps and front delts. That's why a muscle's weekly total can land on a half (e.g. 9.5): it's adding up full sets from direct work and half-sets from assistance. |
| **Frequency** | How often you train a muscle across the week, e.g. "chest twice a week." |
| **Mesocycle** | A training block of roughly 4–8 weeks that builds up and then eases off before you repeat. |
| **Deload** | A planned easy week (lighter weights or fewer sets) that lets accumulated fatigue clear so you come back fresh. |
| **MEV (Minimum Effective Volume)** | The least weekly volume that still grows a muscle — a rough planning landmark, not an exact number. |
| **MRV (Maximum Recoverable Volume)** | The most weekly volume you can do and still recover from. Beyond it, more sets stop helping. |

## Diet phases

| Term | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Bulk** | Eating in a calorie surplus (more than you burn) to support muscle gain, usually accepting some fat gain too. |
| **Cut** | Eating in a calorie deficit (less than you burn) to lose fat while trying to keep muscle. |
| **Maintenance** | Eating about the same calories you burn, so weight stays roughly steady. |
| **Recomposition ("recomp")** | Slowly building muscle and losing fat at the same time, usually near maintenance calories — realistic mainly for beginners and returning lifters. |

## Common mistakes

- **Reading "3x8" as 8 sets of 3.** Sets come first: `3x8` is 3 sets of 8 reps.
- **Counting warm-ups as sets.** A "set" here means a *hard working set*; the lighter warm-up sets don't count toward volume.
- **Treating DOMS as the goal.** Soreness is just a side effect, not proof you trained well — you can grow without it and be sore without progress.
- **Mixing up strength and hypertrophy.** They overlap but aren't identical: strength is how much you can lift, hypertrophy is how big the muscle gets.
- **Assuming "to failure" is always required.** Most sets are best stopped a rep or two shy of failure (1–3 RIR), not ground into the ground. **[Grade C]**
- **Treating MEV/MRV as exact numbers.** They're rough planning landmarks that vary a lot between people, not fixed measurements. **[Grade C]**
