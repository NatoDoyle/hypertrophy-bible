# Pillar 09 — Getting Started

**If you have never set foot in a gym, start here.** The other nine pillars are the science of building
muscle; this one is the on-ramp — what a gym is, how to use the equipment, how to stay safe, how to
read a workout, and how to get through the door when the whole thing feels intimidating. You do not
need to understand any of the science to begin. Read a few pages here, do your first session, and let
the rest come with time.

**Status:** ✅ Complete

## The full arc — where you are, and where you're going

You can go from "never trained" to close to your genetic potential. It takes years, not weeks, but the
path is clear. Here's the map, with what to focus on at each stage and how to know you've moved on.

| Stage | You are… | Focus on | You've graduated when… |
|-------|----------|----------|------------------------|
| **0 — Before you start** | Never trained, maybe nervous | Choosing a gym, learning the equipment, [beating the anxiety](overcoming-gym-anxiety.md) | You've walked in and done [your first session](your-first-session.md) |
| **1 — First month** | Brand new | Technique, the habit, learning what "close to failure" feels like | You can set up and perform the main lifts with control, and you're training consistently |
| **2 — Novice** | A few months in | Simple [progressive overload](../03-programming/periodization-and-progression.md) — add reps/weight most sessions | Easy weekly gains slow down (~6–12 months in) |
| **3 — Intermediate** | ~1–3 years | Deliberate [volume](../01-training-variables/volume.md), ~2×/week [frequency](../01-training-variables/frequency.md), [deloads](../05-recovery/deloads-and-rest-days.md) | Progress needs planning, not just showing up |
| **4 — Advanced** | 3+ years | Higher volume, [specialization](../03-programming/weak-point-prioritization.md), [long-term planning](../03-programming/long-term-and-annual-planning.md) | Gains are slow and hard-won |
| **5 — Near your ceiling** | Many years in | [Breaking advanced plateaus](../07-tracking/breaking-advanced-plateaus.md), patience, [realistic expectations](../06-individualization/reaching-your-genetic-ceiling.md) | You're chasing small yearly increments toward your genetic limit |

Most people are **much further from their ceiling than they think** — which is good news: there's a lot
of muscle to build. Don't rush the early stages; the fundamentals here are what make the later ones possible.
