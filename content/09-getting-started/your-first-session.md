# Your First Session

> **TL;DR** — Your first workout is mostly about showing up and moving through the room without stress. Here is the whole thing, minute by minute: arrive, stash your stuff, do a few minutes of easy movement to warm up, find your first exercise, ramp up to a working weight, do your sets with a rest between each, then move on and repeat. Wipe your equipment down, and leave. Nobody is watching you, everyone was new once, and you cannot do this "wrong" as long as you move under control and stay comfortable.

**Quick recommendations**
- **Arrive a few minutes early** so you're not rushed; give yourself permission to move slowly. **[Grade D]**
- Do a **3–5 minute general warm-up** (easy cardio or light movement) before touching a working weight. **[Grade D]**
- Pick your working weight for each exercise with the **ramp-up method** — start light, add a little each set. **[Grade D]**
- **Rest about 1–3 minutes** between hard sets; longer for big compound lifts, shorter for small isolation work. **[Grade C]**
- **Wipe down equipment** after you use it and re-rack your weights — that's the whole etiquette rulebook for day one. **[Grade D]**

## Before you go

Wear anything you can move in and closed-toe shoes. Bring a water bottle and, if you can, a written plan — even three or four exercises jotted on your phone. Having a plan means you never have to stand in the middle of the gym wondering what to do next, which is the single most common source of first-day nerves.

## Minute by minute

**Arrive and settle in (first ~5 minutes).** Walk in, scan your membership or check in, and put your bag, coat, and phone in a locker or a cubby. You now have nothing to carry and nothing to worry about.

**General warm-up (~3–5 minutes).** Get on any cardio machine — treadmill, bike, whatever's free — or just do some easy dynamic movement (arm swings, bodyweight squats, walking). The goal is to raise your body temperature and break a very light sweat, not to tire yourself out. See [warm-ups](../03-programming/warm-up.md) for the reasoning.

**Find your first exercise.** Walk to the machine, rack, or bench for exercise #1 on your plan. If a machine has a diagram or a QR code, read it. If you're unsure how a piece of equipment works or how to set the seat, it is completely fine to look it up or ask a staff member — that's what they're there for.

**Ramp-up sets.** Before your hard sets, do a couple of light sets that climb toward a challenging weight. On your very first session these double as your calibration: start with the lightest option, do your target reps, notice how many you had left, and add a little each set. (Full method: [choosing your starting weight](choosing-your-starting-weight.md).)

**Working sets.** Once a weight leaves the last rep hard but clean — about **2–3 RIR** (reps in reserve: how many more you could have done) — that's your working weight. Do the number of sets your plan lists. Move under control; you should be able to pause the weight at any point. There is no need to grind or strain on day one.

**Rest between sets.** Sit or stand, breathe, sip water, and let yourself recover for roughly **1–3 minutes** between hard sets — nearer 2–3 minutes on big lifts like squats or presses, nearer 1 minute on small isolation moves. Resting is part of the workout, not slacking. More detail in [rest periods](../01-training-variables/rest-periods.md).

**Move to the next exercise.** Repeat the ramp-up-then-working-sets pattern for each exercise on your plan. Re-rack any weights and wipe machines as you leave them.

**Finish and leave.** When your plan's done, you're done. Wipe down your last station, grab your things, and head out. That's a complete session.

## Reassurance, because you need it

- **Nobody is watching you.** Regulars are focused on their own sets. A beginner ramping up light barely registers.
- **You will not do it "wrong."** As long as you move under control and stop short of failure, a lighter-than-optimal weight still builds the habit and the skill.
- **Asking for help is normal.** Staff expect equipment questions. Asking is a sign you're being sensible, not a sign you don't belong.
- **Short is fine.** A first session of 20–40 minutes is plenty. You're establishing a pattern, not setting records.

## Common mistakes

- **Skipping the plan.** Wandering with no list is what makes the gym feel intimidating. Even a rough list fixes it.
- **Going too heavy to look experienced.** Day one is for calibration, not maxing. Light and clean beats heavy and shaky.
- **Not resting enough.** Rushing between sets tanks your performance and makes everything feel harder than it is.
- **Forgetting to wipe down / re-rack.** It's the one bit of etiquette people notice. Two seconds, and you're a model gym citizen.
- **Judging the whole experience by day one.** The first session is the awkward one. It gets easier fast.
