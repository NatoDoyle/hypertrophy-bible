# Pain and Warning Signs

> **TL;DR** — Hard training is *supposed* to feel effortful: a burning muscle during a set and dull, achy soreness a day or two later are **normal**. What is **not** normal — and means **stop the set immediately** — is sharp or stabbing **joint** pain, **chest** pain or pressure, **dizziness**, **numbness or tingling**, a sudden **pop** with pain, or **breathlessness far beyond the effort**. When in doubt, rack the weight and sit down. This page is **educational, not medical advice**.

**Quick recommendations**
- Learn the difference between **muscular burn/effort/next-day soreness** (fine) and **joint, chest, or nerve symptoms** (stop). **[Grade D]**
- If anything on the red-flag list appears, **rack or set down the weight mid-rep** — don't finish the set. **[Grade D]**
- For **chest pain/pressure, breathlessness, fainting, or one-sided weakness or numbness**, treat it as an **emergency** and call emergency services (in the US, **911**). **[Grade D]**
- If you are **sedentary, older, pregnant, or medically complex**, get **medical clearance before you start** training. **[Grade D]**
- **Don't train through** sharp joint pain or a sudden pop — stop and get it assessed. **[Grade D]**

## Normal discomfort (keep going)

These are the ordinary sensations of productive training:

- **Muscular burn** during a set — a hot, building ache in the working muscle from metabolite buildup. It fades within a minute of racking.
- **Effort and strain** — a hard set *should* feel hard: heavy breathing, a grimace, shaking on the last reps.
- **DOMS** (delayed-onset muscle soreness) — dull, achy, **symmetrical** tenderness in the muscle belly that shows up **12–72 hours** after new or harder training and eases with light movement. It is a normal response to unfamiliar work, not a sign of damage you need to fear.

Note the pattern: normal discomfort is **in the muscle**, **diffuse**, and **fades**. Red flags are **sharp**, **in a joint or elsewhere**, or **systemic** (whole-body: faint, breathless).

## Red flags — stop now

| Symptom | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Sharp, stabbing, or localized joint pain** | Not muscle burn — a joint, tendon, or ligament may be at risk. |
| **A sudden pop or snap with pain**, or sudden loss of strength | Possible tear or rupture. |
| **Chest pain or pressure**, or pain spreading to arm, jaw, or back | Possible cardiac event — a medical emergency. |
| **Dizziness, lightheadedness, or feeling faint** | Blood pressure/circulation problem; you may pass out under a load. |
| **Numbness or tingling**, especially down an arm or leg | Possible nerve involvement. |
| **Shortness of breath out of proportion** to the effort | Not normal exertion — treat as a warning. |
| **Sudden severe headache, blurred vision, or nausea** with the above | Systemic warning — stop and seek help. |

## What to do

1. **Stop the rep.** Rack the bar or set the weight down immediately — do not try to finish. (See [failing and bailing safely](spotting-failing-and-bailing-safely.md) for how to ditch a loaded bar.)
2. **Sit or lie down**, especially with dizziness or chest symptoms, so a faint can't drop you under the weight.
3. **Escalate by symptom.** For **chest pain or pressure, breathlessness, fainting, sudden one-sided weakness or numbness, or a sudden severe headache**, call **emergency services (911 in the US)** — **don't drive yourself.** For a **pop or sharp joint pain**, stop training that area and get it assessed before loading it again.
4. **Tell gym staff.** Gyms carry first-aid supplies and often an AED (automated defibrillator); you don't have to handle it alone.

## Get cleared first if this is you

Talk to a doctor **before starting** if you are **sedentary/new to exercise and over ~40**, **pregnant**, or **medically complex** — for example heart disease, high or uncontrolled blood pressure, diabetes, a recent surgery or injury, or any condition your doctor watches. Clearance is a quick, sensible step, not a barrier; most people are told to go ahead, often with minor tailoring.

*Reminder: this is general education, not a diagnosis or medical advice. If symptoms worry you, treat them seriously and seek professional care.*

## Common mistakes

- **Treating sharp joint pain as "pushing through it."** Burn is fine to push; sharp joint pain is not.
- **Ignoring dizziness or chest symptoms** because the set "felt strong" — these are emergencies regardless of how motivated you feel.
- **Confusing normal DOMS with injury** and skipping training you could do — sore-but-diffuse muscle usually trains fine with lighter effort.
- **Finishing the rep anyway** when a red flag appears — put the weight down first, always.
- **Skipping medical clearance** when you're older, pregnant, or managing a condition.
- **Holding your breath and straining maximally** when lightheaded — breathe, and stop if the room spins.
