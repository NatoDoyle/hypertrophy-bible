## The one-paragraph model

Hard contractions against meaningful resistance create **mechanical tension**, which muscle fibers
sense and answer by increasing **protein synthesis**; over weeks of synthesis outpacing breakdown,
fibers add contractile protein and enlarge, often supported by adding **myonuclei** from satellite
cells. Each session is both a growth **stimulus** and a source of **fatigue**; adaptation is realized
during **recovery**. Do enough of this, progressively, for long enough, and you build muscle — faster
as a beginner, slower as you approach your genetic ceiling.
